Life Insurance Corporation of India: A Comprehensive Overview #

About the Company #

Year of Establishment and Founding History #

Life Insurance Corporation of India (LIC) was established on September 1, 1956. It was formed by merging and nationalizing 245 private life insurance companies in India, aiming to provide widespread insurance coverage and mobilize public savings for national development.

Headquarters Location and Global Presence #

LIC’s headquarters are located in Mumbai, India at Yogakshema Building, Jeevan Bima Marg. While primarily focused on the Indian market, LIC also has a presence in several international markets including:

  • United Kingdom: LIC (UK) Limited
  • Mauritius: LIC (Mauritius) Offshore
  • Bahrain: Branch Office
  • Fiji: Branch Office
  • Nepal: LIC Nepal Limited
  • Singapore: Representative Office

Company Vision and Mission #

  • Vision: To be a globally competitive financial conglomerate and a model corporate citizen.
  • Mission: Ensure and enhance the financial well-being of people through efficient mobilization of their savings and providing need-based life insurance and related products, managed by prudent investments, and delivered by customer-centric processes.

Key Milestones in Their Growth Journey #

  • 1956: Nationalization of 245 private life insurance companies and establishment of LIC.
  • 2000: End of LIC’s monopoly with the opening up of the insurance sector to private players.
  • 2006: Golden Jubilee Celebrations.
  • 2022: Initial Public Offering (IPO) and listing on the stock exchanges.

Stock Exchange Listing Details and Market Capitalization #

LIC is listed on both the National Stock Exchange (NSE) and the Bombay Stock Exchange (BSE). As of October 26, 2023, LIC’s market capitalization is approximately ₹429881.18 Cr.

Recent Financial Performance Highlights (Based on 2022-23 Annual Report) #

  • Total Income: ₹776,963 Crore
  • Net Profit After Tax (PAT): ₹3,632.42 Crore
  • Total Assets Under Management (AUM): ₹43,97,205 Crore
  • Market Share in First Year Premium: 62.58%

Management Team and Leadership Structure #

The LIC is headed by a Chairman, supported by Managing Directors and Executive Directors responsible for various functional areas.

  • Chairman: Shri Siddhartha Mohanty

Notable Awards or Recognitions #

  • “India’s Most Trusted Private Life Insurer” by Reader’s Digest Trusted Brand Awards.
  • Various awards and recognitions for its CSR initiatives and contributions to social welfare.

Their Products #

Complete Product Portfolio with Categories #

LIC offers a wide range of life insurance products catering to diverse needs and financial goals. Key product categories include:

  • Individual Assurance Plans: Endowment Plans, Term Assurance Plans, Whole Life Plans, Money Back Plans, Micro Insurance Plans.
  • Pension Plans: Annuity Plans, Deferred Annuity Plans.
  • Unit Linked Insurance Plans (ULIPs): Plans that offer market-linked returns along with life cover.
  • Group Schemes: Group Term Insurance, Group Gratuity, Group Superannuation.
  • Health Insurance Plans: Plans that offer health coverage along with life cover.
  • Child Plans

Flagship or Signature Product Lines #

  • Jeevan Anand: A traditional endowment plan offering life cover with returns at maturity and continued risk coverage.
  • Jeevan Labh: A limited premium paying plan with guaranteed additions.

Primary Customers #

Geographic Markets (Domestic vs. International) #

LIC’s primary market is India. It also operates in several international locations through branch offices and subsidiaries.

Major Client Segments #

LIC caters to a broad spectrum of customers including:

  • Salaried individuals
  • Business owners
  • Rural population
  • High net worth individuals (HNIs)
  • Corporates (for group schemes)

Distribution Network and Sales Channels #

LIC has a vast distribution network comprising:

  • Individual Agents: The backbone of LIC’s sales force, with over a million agents across India.
  • Corporate Agents: Partnerships with banks, financial institutions, and other organizations.
  • Brokers: Independent insurance brokers who sell LIC policies.
  • Direct Marketing Channels: Online sales through the LIC website, telemarketing, and direct mail.

Major Competitors #

Direct Competitors in India #

  • HDFC Life Insurance
  • ICICI Prudential Life Insurance
  • SBI Life Insurance
  • Max Life Insurance
  • Bajaj Allianz Life Insurance

Comparative Market Share Analysis #

LIC holds a dominant market share in the Indian life insurance industry, although private players have been steadily increasing their presence. LIC retains the majority share in terms of number of policies sold.

How They Differentiate from Competitors #

  • Brand Trust and Legacy: LIC enjoys immense brand trust and a long-standing reputation in India.
  • Widespread Reach: LIC has a vast network of branches and agents, particularly in rural areas, giving it unmatched reach.
  • Sovereign Guarantee: LIC policies are backed by a sovereign guarantee, providing a high level of security to policyholders.

Industry Challenges and Opportunities #

  • Increasing Competition: Private players are becoming increasingly competitive, offering innovative products and services.
  • Low Insurance Penetration: India still has a relatively low insurance penetration rate, providing significant growth potential.
  • Technological Disruption: Digitalization and technology are transforming the insurance industry, requiring LIC to adapt and innovate.

Future Outlook #

Expansion Plans or Growth Strategy #

  • Focus on increasing insurance penetration in rural and underserved areas.
  • Expanding digital channels and leveraging technology to improve customer experience.
  • Developing new and innovative products to meet the evolving needs of customers.

Sustainability Initiatives or ESG Commitments #

LIC is increasingly focusing on ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance) factors in its investment decisions and operations.

Industry Trends Affecting Their Business #

  • Digitalization: The growing adoption of digital channels and technology is transforming the insurance industry.
  • Increasing Awareness: Rising awareness of the importance of insurance is driving demand for life insurance products.
  • Aging Population: The aging population is creating a greater need for pension and retirement planning products.

Long-Term Vision and Strategic Goals #

  • Maintain its position as the leading life insurer in India.
  • Enhance customer satisfaction and build stronger relationships with policyholders.
  • Contribute to the economic development of India through prudent investment and social welfare initiatives.

LIC Performance Overview: A Comprehensive Analysis #

3-Year Trend Analysis of Key Financial Metrics #

  • Total Premium: Increased marginally by 0.22% from FY 2022-23 to FY 2023-24.
  • Net Profit After Tax (PAT): Increased from ₹36,397.39 crore in FY 2022-23 to ₹40,675.79 crore in FY 2023-24.
  • Assets Under Management (AUM): Grew by 16.48%, from ₹43,97,205 crore as of March 31, 2023, to ₹51,21,887 crore as of March 31, 2024.
  • Indian Embedded Value (IEV): Increased significantly by 24.92%, from ₹5,82,243 crore in FY 2022-23 to ₹7,27,344 crore in FY 2023-24.
  • Value of New Business (VNB): Increased from ₹9,156 crore in FY 2022-23 to ₹9,583 crore in FY 2023-24.
  • VNB Margin: Improved from 16.2% to 16.8%.
  • Solvency Ratio: Improved from 1.87 as of March 31, 2023, to 1.98 as of March 31, 2024.
  • 13th-Month Persistency Ratio (Premium Basis): Increased from 77.09% in FY 2022-23 to 77.66% in FY 2023-24.
  • 61st-Month Persistency Ratio (Premium Basis): Decreased from 61.80% in FY 2022-23 to 60.88% in FY 2023-24.
  • Expense of Management Ratio: Increased marginally from 15.53% in FY 2022-23 to 15.57% in FY 2023-24.
  • Commission Ratio: Increased from 5.39% in FY 2022-23 to 5.46% in FY 2023-24.

Business Segment Performance #

  • Individual Business: Non-participating segment showed strong growth of 21.79%, with premium increasing from ₹17,778 crore in FY 2022-23 to ₹21,652 crore in FY 2023-24. Unit-linked segment also demonstrated strong growth of 32.19%

Major Strategic Initiatives and Progress #

  • Introduced new non-participating products to strengthen the product basket.
  • Initiated Project DIVE (Digital Innovation and Value Enhancement) to enhance digital engagement and customer experience.
  • Focused on leveraging the extensive agent network and strengthening digital channels.

Risk Landscape Changes #

  • The risk landscape for LIC revolves around human capital, responsible investing, data privacy and security, access to finance, and emerging risks.
  • Specific risks mentioned include operational risks from process and system failures, cybersecurity, and compliance.

ESG Initiatives and Metrics #

  • The Corporation is formalizing an ESG framework.
  • Initiatives include reducing paper usage, installing solar PV panels (6575 KW capacity across 148 locations, leading to a reduction of 5453.96 tons of carbon annually).
  • Promoting use of EV’s (Electric Vehicle) among staff, with 29 lakh trees planted.
  • Social initiatives include funding of projects through LIC Golden Jubilee Foundation, focusing on poverty alleviation, education, and healthcare.

Management Outlook #

  • Focus on profitable growth in market share, value creation for all stakeholders, and enhancing customer experience through digital services and innovative products.
  • Plans for intensifying efforts to increase market presence and deploy resources for continuous development.

Comparative Analysis with Industry Averages #

  • Market Share: LIC maintains a dominant market share of 58.87% in the total life insurance business.

Audit and Regulatory Analysis #

Auditor’s Opinion and Qualifications #

  • The auditors issued an unmodified opinion on the standalone and consolidated financial statements.
  • No qualifications, reservations, or adverse remarks were reported by the auditors.

Key Accounting Policies and Changes #

  • Financial statements are prepared under the historical cost convention, on an accrual basis, and in compliance with generally accepted accounting principles in India.
  • The accounting policies of foreign subsidiaries are aligned based on local laws and regulations. Material differences, where applicable, are disclosed.
  • Premium income is recognized when due, with specific provisions for linked business.
  • Investment income components, including interest, dividends, and rent, are accounted for on an accrual basis.
  • Investments are valued based on specific guidelines for each category (e.g., debt securities at amortized cost, listed equities at fair value).
  • Changes in the method of accounting for Policyholders’ funds were made regarding transfers from Non-Participating Policyholders account to Shareholders’ account, as detailed.
  • Depreciation on fixed assets are calculated using the straight-line method, based on estimated useful life of assets.

Internal Control Effectiveness #

  • The Corporation maintains internal financial controls over financial reporting, designed to ensure reliability and compliance.
  • Internal audit functions and systems are in place to monitor the adequacy and operating effectiveness of internal controls.

Regulatory Compliance Status #

  • The Corporation states compliance with the provisions of the Life Insurance Corporation Act, 1956, the Insurance Act, 1938, the IRDA Act, 1999, and other applicable regulations and guidelines.
  • Compliance with SEBI Listing Regulations and Secretarial Standards is affirmed.
  • No instances of non-compliance relating to capital markets were reported during the relevant period.

Legal Proceedings and Their Potential Impact #

  • The Corporation has disclosed pending litigations impacting its financial position, as specified in the financial statements.
  • There were no significant legal proceedings or regulatory actions that would materially impact the Corporation’s status as a going concern.
  • One pending writ petition is with Supreme Court.

Related Party Transactions #

  • Material related party transactions were entered into in the ordinary course of business and were approved by the Audit Committee.
  • Details of related party transactions and balances are disclosed in the notes to the financial statements.

Subsequent Events #

  • No material changes or commitments affecting the financial position of the Corporation occurred between the end of the financial year and the date of the report.
