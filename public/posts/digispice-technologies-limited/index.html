DiGiSPICE Technologies Limited: Earnings Call Transcript Analysis

February 17, 2025   ·   4 min read

#DiGiSPICE Technologies Limited #Earnings Call

Earnings Call Transcript Analysis Report #

  • Revenue: Grew 7% quarter-on-quarter and 2% year-over-year in Q3 FY25.

  • Gross Margin: Increased by 3% quarter-on-quarter from Rs. 43 crores to Rs. 44 crores, and 5% year-over-year.

  • EBITDA: Improved from (-3.2) crores to (-1.9) crores quarter-on-quarter.

  • EBIT: Improved from Rs. 40 lakhs in the previous quarter to Rs. 2.8 crores.

  • PAT (Continued Business): Positive at Rs. 1 crore, compared to (-1.5) crores in the previous quarter.

  • Overall PAT: Rs. 10 lakh positive, compared to (-1.6) crores in the previous quarter.

  • YTD PAT (Continued & Discontinued Business): Improved from (-25.7) crores in the previous nine months to (-1) crore in the current nine months.

  • Customer GTV: Increased from 26,258 Cr to 31,951 Cr showing the growth of 16%

  • Notional Gain/Loss on Investments: After this loss, PAT is (-26.6) crores for 9 months and (-25.7) crores in the previous 9 months.

  • Collections GTV: Contributed 46-47% of total volumes, nearly equal to AePS and Micro ATM business. Grew 80% year on year

  • Subscription Packs Revenue: Grew approximately 80% year-on-year, from 2.5 crores to 4.5 crores.

  • Float Income: Generated from a balance of 130 crores maintained in opened accounts.

  • Focus: Building a leading FinTech platform under the brand Spice Money, focusing on digital financial inclusion in rural India.

  • New Product Launch: Spice Pay, a UPI offering, launched in beta in Bihar and Madhya Pradesh.

  • Strategic NBFC Acquisition: Application filed with the regulator for an NBFC acquisition within the group.

  • Amalgamation of Spice Money: Process underway to merge Spice Money into DiGiSPICE Technologies.

  • Consolidating Core Digital business and maintain Market Leadership.

  • Doubling Down on operating leverage in the core business.

  • Focusing on Strategic products like savings and investments.

  • Merchant Network Growth: Grew from 240,000 merchants in FY ‘20 to over 1.5 million by December 2024 (55% CAGR).

  • AePS Market Leadership: Maintained a market share of above 17% in the Aadhaar-enabled payment system (AePS) business, nearing 18%.

  • UPI Market Entry: Committed to expanding the UPI consumer base, targeting the next 300 million users from Bharat.

  • State-by-state strategy to increase the AePS market share

  • Regional Presence: Strong presence in North, Central, and East India, deepening presence in West and South India.

  • Collections Business Growth: Collections business grew by 80% year-on-year, becoming a significant part of the story.

  • BBPS: Flat growth this quarter due to the seasonality.

  • GST Notice: Received a GST notice related to export services in the discontinued business, with the company hopeful for a favorable resolution.

  • Fraud Control: Mentioned the need to closely watch fraud-to-sales ratios in a financially vulnerable market.

  • Regulatory Changes: Acknowledged the impact of transaction limits and two-factor authentication in the AePS space.

  • UPI Expansion: Goal to onboard many consumers onto UPI from the existing network and expand to 300 million users.

  • Spice Pay Development: Plan to build Spice Pay into a full-stack financial services platform, adding savings, investments, insurance, and credit products.

  • Credit Business: Credit is identified as a crucial part of the future journey, with plans to create credit products for the captive merchant base and MSMEs.

  • Geographical Expansion-Deepening Presence in the west and south of India.

  • AePS Market Share Strategy: Highlighted a state-by-state strategy to increase AePS market share, aiming for 20%+ overall. “We are looking at a state-by-state strategy of how to grow AePS market share.”

  • CMS GTV Growth Drivers: Confirmed that the growth in CMS GTV is due to both an increase in large SMEs and growth in business per enterprise.

  • Impact of UPI Adoption: Management views zero-balance savings accounts and PPI wallets as enablers for digital payments and expects subsequent revenue growth.

  • Expansion into Fintech Verticals: Confirmed plans to leverage AI and collaborate with partners for insurance, wealth management, and other services.

  • Digital Lending Strategy: Emphasized the commitment to driving formal credit penetration to small merchants using alternate data.

  • Commissions for Adhikari- Management confirms that with Subscriptions, Adhikaris stay longer.

  • Margin Pressure: Acknowledged that the growth in the collections business, a lower-margin product, is impacting overall GM growth relative to GTV growth.

  • WhatsApp’s Market Entry and Spice Money’s positioning- Spice Money is an interoperable with UPI, where one can add cash and Scan and Pay, and other payments.

  • Overall Tone: Confident and optimistic about the future, emphasizing the focus on digital financial inclusion and growth in Bharat.

  • Confidence Areas: Particularly confident about the AePS market leadership, the growth in the collections business, and the potential of Spice Pay.

  • Concern Areas: Acknowledged challenges related to fraud control and regulatory changes but expressed confidence in managing them.

  • Cautious but determined regarding the ongoing GST issue.
